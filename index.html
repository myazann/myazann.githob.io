About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at Hogeschool van Amsterdam and Leiden University, focusing on the fascinating intersection of chatbot technology, consumer psychology, and personalization in low-resource scenarios. My research aims to enhance user experiences by developing chatbots that can adapt their conversation styles based on context.

My work explores two main areas:

  1. Improving knowledge transfer of chatbots to low-resource domains
  2. Aligning chatbot behavior with consumer psychology to enhance adaptability in specific domains

Recent projects include investigating the impact of chatbot conversation styles on customer risk perception and acceptance, as well as analyzing how quantization affects the retrieval-augmented generation capabilities of smaller Large Language Models (LLMs). My future research aims to further develop methods for LLM personalization and demonstrate its positive impact on user satisfaction and recommendation quality.

Prior to my PhD, I earned a Master's degree in Computer Vision from Universitat Autonoma de Barcelona and a Bachelor's in Computer Engineering from Galatasaray University. I also gained valuable industry experience as a Data Scientist at Fibabanka, where I worked on credit scoring and fraud detection systems.
